About Me



Allow me to properly introduce myself. My name is Fred Berberich. Currently, I am about to enter my senior year of college at Marist. Since I can remember, I've always had a pull towards technology, but it wasn't until I started attending Marist where I met many different professors and friends who gave me so many experiences in this field. I wanted to work on a project that bettered my coding skills because I find that no matter which part of Computer Science, Cybersecurity, or even IT, I choose to pursue, it seems having stronger coding skills never hurts to have.


Through my time as a lab assistant working in a networking lab, I have gained hands on experience with configuring, setting up, and repairing, Cisco switches and routers. I tutored students on the various network protocols and practices covered in Marist's Internetworking class, and helped installed new equipment in the lab. Aside from working directly with hardware and with my classmates, I've been able to build my networking skills through configuring many virtual networks using VMware running Linux and Windows based systems.


My research on Low Energy Bluetooth also inhanced both my knowledge in Cybersecurity and IT. Studying Bluetooth mesh networks that are created and used through Low Energy Bluetooth procots (BLE), gave me a wider understanding on how and where networks are being used on a daily basis. Studying the security protocols and how the BLE functions expanded my knowledge on how hackers could exploit other types of networks we use that we don't even think of to attack our devices.


Aside from the works listed in my "Projects" page, there are some additional information I'd like to give about myself. I am Vice President of Marist's Cybersecurity Club, where we cover lesson topics on cybersecurity, spread cyber awerness to those who may not be well informed on security when using the internet, and even try to network members with those who are already in the field. I will also be starting my position as a ECRL Systems Administrator in the Fall 2024 semster with Marist's joint study with IBM. I am extremely excited to start my position as I'm sure I will get to gain more knowledge and skills with IBM products and machinery.
